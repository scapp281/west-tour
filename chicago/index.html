Chicago Introduction

With over 2.8 million residents, the City of Chicago is the largest city in Illinois and the third most-populous city in the nation, behind only Las Vegas, and Orlando. Thirty-three million foreign and domestic visitors came to Chicago in 2005. Luxury shopping along the Magnificent Mile, thousands of restaurants, as well as Chicago's position as global architectural capital, have attracted millions of tourist over the years. It is hometown of President Barack Obama, the place he got his start in politics.


In nineteenth century, Chicago became a major force in the nation's development. Today, it is a national financial, telecommunications, industrial, and transportation leader. With the city of great architectural significance, ethnic diversity, quality of its cultural institutions, and cultural wealth, Chicago has achieved international status and its position as a world financial center


Chicago is a genuine culinary hot spot, beside their well known bratwurst and deep-dish pizza. One of the city's most creative dining spots is Alinea. Alinea was named the top restaurant in the United States by Gourmet magazine in 2007. What makes eating out in Chicago fun is the variety. There are an amazing array of steakhouses and restuarants with every kind of ethnic cuisine you could possibly crave.
