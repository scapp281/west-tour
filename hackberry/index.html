Painted Desert

A broad region of rocky badlands, encompassing more than 93,500 acres and stretches over 160 miles, features rocks in every hue from deep lavenders and rich grays to reds, oranges and even pinks. It took millions upon millions of years for nature to create this natural canvas of unimaginable design of "multi-colored layered cake". Visit Tour

Petrified Forest National Park is a surprising realm of fascinating landscape and science. Experience one of the world’s largest and most vibrantly colored assemblies of petrified wood, historic structures and archeological sites at Petrified Forest. On first impression, the Petrified Forest may not sound very appealing as it comprises stone log fragments scattered over a featureless section of Arizona desert.

But here you can find 200-million-year-old fossils tell the tale of the earth and its prehistoric inhabitants. There are scenic hiking trails and beautiful campgrounds such as Red Basin and little known areas like the Devil's Playground. There are new exhibits to bring the stories to life. Come rediscover Petrified Forest!


A must-see for nature lovers, picturesque wonders of Arizona’s Painted Desert. Home to many memorable formations and features, which serves as proof of the earth’s volatility: volcanic eruptions, earthquakes, floods and sunlight. That all combined to create the Painted Desert. Deposits of clay and sandstone, stacked in elegant layers, play with the setting Arizona sun in an altering display of colorful radiance.
