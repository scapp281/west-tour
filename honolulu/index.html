Honolulu Introduction

Honolulu is the state capital and the most populous city of Hawaii. Honolulu is situated on the island of Oahu. Hawaii is a major tourist destination. While Honolulu is the main gateway to Hawaii and a major gateway into the United States. The city is also a major hub for international business, military defense.


Honolulu is both the westernmost and the southernmost major American city. As a cosmopolitan city, Honolulu famously being host to a diverse variety of east-west and Pacific culture, cuisine, and traditions. The city is the principal port for the Hawaiian Islands and an important center for military defense with several bases, including Pearl Harbor NavalGained historical recognition following the attack on Pearl Harbor by Japan in 1941. It serves as the crossroads of the Pacific Ocean with ship and air connections to the U.S. mainland, Asia, Australia, and New Zealand.


Here you’ll find everything from historic landmarks and treasured monuments to world-class shopping and a flourishing arts and culture scene. Historic landmarks, include but not limit to Iolani Palace, the Kawaiahao Church, the Hawaiian Mission Houses Historic Site and Archives. Honolulu is also Hawaii’s hot spot for arts, culture and entertainment.


From the nightlife, live music and fine dining of Waikiki to the art galleries and underground bars of the Chinatown arts district. Whether you’re looking for Hawaii’s finest museums, or Hawaii’s finest Hawaii Regional Cuisine chefs, the best resorts, festivals, and events, or just some fun things to do, you’ll find it all in Honolulu.
