Joplin Museum Complex

Home to several museums. Each of the museums in the complex has life exhibits, interactive displays, and extensive showcases of artifacts address positive and negative effects that mining had on Joplin. The overall goal of the complex is pass on the stories of early days of mining life in the Tri-State district. Visit Tour

Joplin Introduction

Joplin is a city located in southern Jasper County and northern Newton County in the southwestern Missouri, encompassing Oklahoma, Kansas, Missouri and Arkansas. Joplin is named for Reverend Harris Joplin, an early settler and the founder of the area. Joplin was established in 1873 and expanded significantly from the wealth created by the mining of zinc.


From a scattering of rough mining camps through the mid-point of 20th Century, Joplin grew virtually overnight to become the world's premier supplier of lead and zinc ore. Because of these raw materials, the city also became a transportation hub and still retains the title of The Crossroads of America. Joplin served as social mecca for the soldiers training at Camp Crowder during World War II.


At the Joplin Museum Complex, located at Fourth Street and Schifferdecker Avenue, a cat greeter welcomes you to the two museums that tell the story of Joplin’s history. The Dorothea B. Hoover Historical Museum displays a variety of historic items, including a 1927 fire engine and the Tri-State Mineral Museum houses one of the world’s largest collections of lead and zinc ore, calcite dog-tooth crystals and other fabulous specimens, as well as mining tools and equipment. These museums provide inspiration for future generations to better understand the economic and social history of Joplin and the Tri-State Mining District. Also see Victorian rooms, textiles, minerals, models and other subjects. The historic Dale's Route 66 Barber Shop sits on the corner of Utica St. and Euclid Avenue, and numerous vintage signs can be seen along or nearby the old Mother Road.
