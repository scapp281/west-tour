Las Vegas Introduction

Las Vegas is a city well known enough that needs no introduction. Since late 1980s, Las Vegas has acquired another identity as a center for business, finance, transportation, and services. With its five-star resorts, world-class resturants and boutiques, spectacular entertainment and nightlife, Las Vegas serves as "Entertainment Capital of the World".


From what have seen on TV or in a movie cannot compare to the first sight of Las Vegas. The surrounding of skylines is hyperreality. Half-size recreation of Eiffel Tower is one of Las Vegas’ premier attractions with amazing views. Las Vegas has long outgrown its adult playground connotation. Other than the luxury casinos, there are magnificent amenities and attractions designed to keep you entertained, satisfied, and satiated. These attractions and skylines will distract vistors from the real world outside with its glittering borders. There is a bit of something for everyone, whether you want a wild party, a relaxing getaway, or something in between. Las Vegas is also surprisingly family-friendly. Hotels have spent millions developing sophisticated indoor amusement rides like the Race for Atlantis to attract kids.


Las Vegas is especially romantic at night with of flashing neon and dazzling lights. Las Vegas shows are as well-known and impressive as the city itself. Las Vegas has become one of the premier shopping destinations in the world for fashionista. There's Caesars Palace and the designer boutiques at The Forum Shops. Shore lovers will adore The Beach at Mandalay Bay. Each day in Las Vegas can be a whole new vacation to visit masquerade balls, magic shows, comedy acts, headliner concerts, theatrical performances, symphonies, live animal exhibits at The Mirage Hotel, and others.
