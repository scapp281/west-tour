One of the two spectacular options at Peach Springs. Many visitors to the Grand Canyon do not know about Havasu Falls. Havasu is one of four major waterfalls in the heart of the Havasupai Tribe. Peach Springs Introduction

The historic US Route 66 brought large numbers of traveler through the town until Interstate 40 was completed in 1978. Being bypassed by Interstate 40 and no connecting roads join the two highways at Peach Springs, the town turned into overnight ghost towns. That’s why Peach Springs, Arizona served as an inspiration for the fictional town Radiator Springs in the Pixar movie Cars


Peach Springs is in Mohave County, Arizona. Start from the trailhead, namely, Hualapai Hilltop, where hikers descend 8 miles tail to the town of Supai. Unfortunately, no roads for vehicles to travel to Supai. You either backpack it or go by horse. In Supai, traveler would see world renowned Havasu Falls and three other waterfalls can be visited. Visiting the falls is not a day trip. It is recommended to plan at least 2 days and an overnight in the campground or the Havasupai Lodge.


Peach Springs lies within the traditional territory of the Hualapai Reservation. Hualapai Reservation area offers hunting, fishing, hiking and camping facilities. The hunting expeditions are world renown. The Tribal Wildlife Department sells a limited number of hunting permits for elk, javelin, deer and desert bighorn sheep. Stay an overnight at Hualapai Lodge, try their fitness center, salt pool and available special packages. Whitewater rafting through the Colorado River.
