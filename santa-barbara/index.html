Santa Barbara Introduction

Santa Barbara is situated on the longest section of the West Coast. Lies between Santa Ynez Mountains and Pacific Ocean. In addition to being a popular tourist and resort destination, the city economy includes a large service sector, education, technology, health care, finance, agriculture, manufacturing, and local government. Santa Barbara is sometimes referred to as "The American Riviera" for its With the pristine city's perfect climate, it's hard to top Santa Barbara for a beautiful weekend on the Pacific Coast.


Some cities grow old and then slowly die. Santa Barbara never grows old, just more elegant and appreciative of its past. Santa Barbara housed the world's largest movie studio during the era of silent film. The destructive earthquake on June 29, 1925 left the downtown in ruins. Santa Barbara rebuilt itself and changed its overall look and feel almost overnight. The downtown was restored in a classic red tiled Spanish-Moorish structure.


There are a number of natural attractions worth checking out. Stroll through the Santa Barbara Botanic Gardens to view breathtaking flora, or hike a scenic nature trail in Los Padres National Forest. Santa Barbara has world class shopping, gourmet restaurants and museums. For a fresh and delicious seafood dinner, the restaurants on Stearn's Wharf offer cuisine with a view. Stearn's Wharf is also a great place to buy Santa Barbara souvenirs.
