Springfield Introduction

Located in scenic southwest Missouri, Springfield is home to a metro population of 450,000, encompassing five counties: Greene, Christian, Webster, Polk, and Dallas. The "Queen City of the Ozarks",Springfield’s economy is based on health care, manufacturing, retail, education and tourism. It is the top 10 city with lowest cost of living. Big city with plentiful and growing job market, great schools, world-class health care, and all the entertainment and cultural options. Affordable home you’ve always wanted. Housing costs are 22 percent lower than the national average. Enjoy life, amid four-season beauty on Ozarks Mountain and just breathe.


With a lively history that includes Wild West shootouts and America’s first telegraph line. Ozarks Mountain is a giant playground for outdoor adventures such as camping, biking, hiking, swimming, boating, fishing, hunting, and water-skiing. Beautiful scenery of rolling hills, pristine sparkling streams and lakes. Zip-line over the forest from the top of an Ozarks Mountain. Ride your motorcycle on the open roads.


Variety of interesting attractions in Springfield. Take a tour at one of many wineries. Sample the goods at the first legal moonshine distillery since Prohibition. Tour a Civil War battlefield. Historic parks offer fantastic settings to unwind with picnics, playgrounds, pools, or just a stroll through the trees.
