Sullivan Introduction

Sullivan is a city that straddles the border of Franklin County and Crawford County in Missouri. The community was named after Stephen and Dorcas Sullivan, who provided land to the railroad. Sullivan offers the business, cultural and recreational amenities of a major urban center while balancing the lifestyle and aesthetic qualities of rural living. Sullivan is a place to live, work and play with a promising future


Here are some fun facts about Sullivan. As of 2014, Sullivan's population is 7,830 people. The city's population is 12.2% college educated and 80.4% have a high school diploma or a GED. Sullivan, MO workers work 39/hrs per week on average. $1,042 a month is the typical owner cost for a home with a mortgage. Homes have 5 rooms on average.


Some of the hisoric places are Meramec State Park Beach Area Historic District, Meramec State Park Lookout House/Observation Tower, Meramec State Park Pump House, Meramec State Park Shelter House
