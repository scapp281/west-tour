Yosemite National Park

First protected in 1864, Yosemite National Park is best known for its waterfalls, but within its nearly 1,200 square miles, you can find deep valleys, grand meadows, ancient giant sequoias, a vast wilderness area, and much more. Visit Tour

Yosemite Village Introduction

What are some of the place to go in Yosemite National Park? Yosemite Valley is world famous for its impressive waterfalls, meadows, cliffs, and unusual rock formations. Yosemite Valley is accessible by car all year. At Mariposa Grove, you find giant sequoias. All of the roads in Yosemite National Park are scenic, but the most famous scenic drive is along the Tioga Road. The road is typically open from late May or early June through sometime in November. Another place for spectacular scenery is Hetch Hetchy, which is the starting point for many less-used wilderness trails.


What is there to do in Yosemite? The best way to experience the 1,100 square miles of the Yosemite Wilderness is to explore it first hand. The Yosemite Wilderness has over 750 miles of trail to explore. Little Yosemite Valley is the most popular area in the Yosemite Wilderness, mainly because it provides easy access to Half Dome. Over 12 miles of paved bike paths are available in Yosemite Valley.


Yosemite National Park is a essential habitat for birds and other 100 recorded species. In there, you could see over 165 species of migrating, wintering, and breeding birds while enjoying your picnic. Many other activities vistors could do such as hiking, horseback riding, rock climbing, skiing, snowboarding,ice skating, swimming, boating, rafting, fishing
